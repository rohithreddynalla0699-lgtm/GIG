The food waste crisis in India

India faces one of the world's largest food waste problems. Here's what research and data tell us — and why immediate action matters.

1
Food waste in India contributes 8-10% of global greenhouse gas emissions
According to the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), wasted food in India generates methane in landfills — a greenhouse gas 25 times more potent than CO₂.
2
40% of food produced in India is wasted across the supply chain
Studies by the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) show that ₹92,000 crores worth of food is wasted annually, affecting both farmers and consumers.
3
Every rescued meal prevents 2.5 kg of CO₂ from entering the atmosphere
Research from the Council on Energy, Environment and Water (CEEW) highlights how food rescue directly reduces climate impact at the consumer level.

What's a Surprise Bag?

A Surprise Bag is a collection of delicious, unsold food from restaurants, bakeries, and stores. You won't know exactly what's inside — but it's always fresh, always good, and always at least half the original price.

✨
Made fresh today
Every item is from the same day. No old or stale food — ever.
🎁
Worth 2-3x what you pay
Bags typically contain ₹300-600 worth of food for just ₹99-249.
🏃
Quick 30-minute pickup window
Reserve in the app, show your 4-digit code at the store, and grab your bag.
💡
Pro tip: Bags vary based on what didn't sell that day. That's the surprise! But stores often hint at typical contents in their listing.
